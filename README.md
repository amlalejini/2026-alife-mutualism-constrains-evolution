# Mutualism can constrain host diversity and evolution

This repository is associated with our 2026 Artificial Life Conference submission, "Mutualism can constrain host diversity and evolution".

## Overview

### Abstract

> Coevolutionary arms races between hosts and parasites is known to promote the evolution of complex traits in hosts.
  However, the effects of mutualistic endosymbionts (symbionts that live inside a host) on host evolution is less well understood.
  Here, we use populations of self-replicating computer programs (digital organisms) to investigate the effects of trait matching mutualisms on host evolution.
  We replicate previous findings showing that parasitic endosymbionts increase host diversity and promote the evolution of complex traits.
  In contrast to the effects of parasitism, we find that mutualism decreases host diversity and can constrain the evolution of their host species by imposing selection pressure for hosts to maintain compatibility with their mutualist partners.
  Under strict trait matching conditions, mutualists in our experiments often undergo a form of egalitarian transition in individuality with their hosts, as they experience evolutionary degredation of their ability to reproduce independent of their host.
  The constraining effects of mutualism depend on several factors: environmental benefits available to hosts that forgo mutualism, strength of mutualistic interactions, and restrictions on host-mutualist compatibility.
